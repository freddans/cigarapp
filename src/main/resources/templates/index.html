Cohiba.
Fidel Castro's Favorite

In this blog post, we are going to be giving our readers an insight into Cohiba; the brand is considered by many to be the most successful of all cigar brands and definitely one of our favourites. Cuban Cigars, since the very beginning have always been held in such high regard. An air of affluence, luxury and class have surrounded the product since its very conception. Many fine cigars have been made from the most delicate tobacco leaves in Cuba's beautiful regions, but one particular brand which is recognised as being the one that does it the best is Cohiba. Cohiba is the flagship brand of Habanos S.A and was initially created in 1966 for the Cuban revolutionary Fidel Castro. During these years, Cohiba Cuban Cigars were made in a secret location, now globally known as the famous El Laguito Factory, and only given as gifts to those in political conduct such as diplomats and heads of state. The Cohiba brand really did begin their journey as an elite cigar brand which was only smoked by those worthy of it.

The very name Cohiba derives from the Indian Taino word for 'tobacco' and is believed to be coined after the bunches of tobacco leaves Columbus first saw the natives of Cuba smoking. As aforementioned during its early years, only those who were of political importance and the select few were given a chance to smoke these fantastic cigars. The mystical properties that surround the cigar are due to the Fidel Castro story. Cohiba's origins are said to date back to the mid-1960s, in which a local torcedor was giving Fidel Castro's bodyguard hand-rolled cigars. Fidel Castro enjoyed them so much that he asked for them to be made for his own personal use. This meant that the El Laguito Factory began producing regular cigars containing the unbranded tobacco blend which would later be known as the world-famous Cohiba. In 1968, the brand was launched, and the cigars produced were manufactured on a very small scale, made from only the best quality materials. The success of these cigars in Cuba meant that Cohiba could expand their production line and make it a full one, making it more available in cigar stores worldwide. This led to the brand launching itself as a premium cigar brand in 1982. Notably, in markets outside of the United States, which led to the various lines that have come out of the brand since.

The leaves used in the Cohiba tobacco blend comes from various districts in the world-famous Vuelta Abajo region. The tobacco plantations used by Cohiba are recognised as Vegas Finas de Primera, which are confirmed by the Cuban Tobacco Institute as the best cigar tobacco growing regions. Zino Davidoff, an expert cigar connoisseur, also believes that the leaves from the districts of San Luis and San Juan y Martinez are the most sought after by cigar manufacturers. Therefore, it is absolutely no wonder the cigars from Cohiba are of high-grade quality and so popular amongst cigar aficionados worldwide. The cigars which contain the seco, ligero and the rare medio tiempo filler leaves have all undergone an additional fermentation process which is what gives the Cohiba brand it's distinctive and very popular flavours.
